## The whole Guangzhou-Shenzhen Intercity Express thingy.

I assume that you have some basic knowledge on how China Railway system works and have some basic idea on CR/CRH EMUs.  
So this railway, first opened in 1911 connecting to Hong Kong got a speed bump and dual-rail upgrade at the turn of the century, which leveled up it's speed to 200km/h max to suit up those crazy high-speed railway plan, and bought a tons of trains for it.  
But right after the crash happened in 2012, they lowered the speed limit to 160km/h, which made it more like an old Electric head pulling 16 carriage speed but still charge you for a premium fee for the ease of transporting between two cities' central in an hour.  

Which leads us to the train I want to talk about today, the fricking CRH1A.  
I used to love this dumb little train, small, runs rather snappy, and with imported parts which somehow means that it could stand against the time; which is a pro in most cases.  
But the thing is this crap is nearly 20 years old at this point, not counting the platform it was based on, Regina by Bombardier.  
And your honour CR/GSR charge me CNY72 on this crap of metal for 120km? nuts.  
Be fair we all knew this is a huge cash cow and we could do nothing about it, and they actually speedbumped it back to 200km/h(TOP) this year, albeit It could only pull 180km/h most of the time due to the schedule table is already packed full at this point.
