## Why you buy yet yet yet yet another ThinkPad, I thought you're retired from all this after buying that M4 MacBook Air.

Well, turns out that I still need some sort of Generic x86-64 Linux portable that is power efficient(no going back after seeing how ARM MacBooks pull when doing stuff).  
Naturally I first turned my attention to my good old ThinkPad A285(aka X280 with a motherboard housing AMD Ryzen Zen+ SoCs and with Thunderbolt 3 support removed), which is not doing good in terms of power efficiency goes, 10W idle... almost 20W when watching YouTube vids, I know that gotta be something wrong with my machine but I just don't want to mess with it so I just decided to grab a new one.  
And AMD laptops in my pov... at least till Zen 3, have a shitty power management stuff, I know it's OEM's fault but I don't want to tackle all this shit so I went for Intel machine this time, even 8th gen would do.  
And one part that play a huge role during my previous acquisition decisions is that GPU should be somehow nice, which no longer matters anymore since I quit PC gaming stuff a long time ago, and even if theres a need, for Minecraft I either use my M4 Mac mini or my Intel B580 rig or just anything else, at least it will works on most of my machines.  

## How do it feels like, is it good?

Really worth it, at least for now, power efficiency wise ofc It can't compete with those crazy ARM chips or modern x86-64 socs, but being something from 7 yrs ago it's something to be respect of, and having a 57Wh battery on board... just crazy  

And it's light weight, which fits my standards, and now maybe 14 inch laptops could be acceptable after this pull it off so nicely done.  

## What will you use if for?

Mostly blog writing I guess, or anything that require a use of *nix based systems that is not macOS, or simple net surfing.  

For media comsumptions, still heading back to my Macs... which have much better speakers and handy AirPods connetivity.  

Pretty nice purchase I gotta say(sad thing for my M4 MacBook Air because this laptop further squashed it's use).  

Tho I will still only do homework stuff on it since it had all the MS Office stuff and fancy fonts that I need to use and collabration tools and shitty software that only macOS could handle it properly(Yes and by that I mean most chinese apps I have to use).  

Let's see how all this holds up in the long run I guess?
